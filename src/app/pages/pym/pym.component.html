What I would have done differently

These are the lessons and learnings I got from working on this project

#1. Designing what the client wants & importance of carrying out UX research initially

Initially I worked on this project while I was fairly new to UI design, and being the only designer, made me realize I should have carried out more UX research and not only designing what the client wants, it could have sped up our designing timeline and haphazardly designing screens that don’t solve the main objective.

#2. Taking more importance to sketching and wireframing

I immediately started with designing the hi-fidelity wireframes, which took up too much time only to be rejected later. I would have instead sketched the designs first and approved with the client before creating the hi-fidelity design.

#3. Prototyping everything wasn't necessarry

I initially assumed I needed to prototype every single screen in order to provide a seamless app flow when presenting to stakeholders. This became time consuming instead of only prototyping screens that aren’t easy to show with only screen flow arrows.
