Background

SageBrief is a web application that assists legal practitioners with faster and more convenient ways of approaching their legal research using AI

Problem Brief

We found that when a lawyer is advising a client on a case, especially one they may not have a wide knowledge of, it can consume much of their time (~ 70% of time) on research, and even then, can potentially provide inaccurate legal advice to their clients.

Prototyping

Exploration/Solutions Considered

We initially wanted to test out non-tech solutions to see if it can be easily solved, but this method did not work out, and we eventually ended up building a web app.

Solution chosen and why

SageBrief Legal is web based research application that provides access to a comprehensive and reliable database of industry-specific corporate and regulatory laws, practice notes, templates, roadmaps and checklists

    We found that this would help startup entrepreneurs focus mainly on their business’ objectives, since it would provide the following values:

  • Centralized research database

  • Expand their knowledge base

  • Faster research time & accuracy

    • Why a web app - By creating a web app, we knew from research, lawyers normally use laptops/desktops while working on different cases/transactions for clients, so creating a web app was the best option.

Solution Validation

We tested our MVP through the following ways:

  • User Testing - We took the prototype through 2 tests with 2 different groups (startup entrepreneurs & lawyers)

  • A/B Testing - In the same 2 different groups, we provide two different versions of the web app prototype, to see which they’d gravitate more to as well as which helps them quickly onboard
